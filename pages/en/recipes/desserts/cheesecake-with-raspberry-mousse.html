Cheesecake with Raspberry Mousse

4 hrs (1 hr active) 12 servings 385 kcal Dessert Cake

Classic Dessert with a Fruity Twist

Cheesecake is one of the most popular desserts, and when paired with refreshing raspberry mousse, it creates a perfect harmony of flavors. The creamy consistency of the cheesecake combined with the slightly tangy, fruity mousse and crispy base is a true feast for the palate.

This impressive dessert will be the centerpiece of any family celebration, holiday table, but also works perfectly as an everyday sweet treat. Preparation requires some time, but the end result definitely compensates for all the effort.

Ingredients

Crust:
  • Butter cookies/graham crackers 250 g
  • Butter (melted) 100 g
Cheese Filling:
  • Cream cheese (Philadelphia) 750 g
  • Sugar 200 g
  • Potato starch 1 tablespoon
  • Eggs 4
  • Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Raspberry Mousse:
  • Raspberries (fresh/frozen) 300 g
  • Powdered sugar 100 g
  • Gelatin 15 g
  • Water 60 ml
  • Lemon juice from 1/2 lemon
  • Heavy cream (36%) 200 ml

Instructions

Crust:
  1. Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F) and prepare a 23 cm (9-inch) springform pan, lining the bottom with parchment paper.
  2. Crush the cookies in a food processor until they become fine crumbs. Add the melted butter and mix thoroughly.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of the pan, spreading it evenly. Place in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.
Cheese Filling:
  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese with sugar until smooth. Add the potato starch and mix.
  2. Add eggs one at a time, mixing at low speed after each addition. Add the vanilla extract and mix.
  3. Pour the cheese mixture onto the chilled crust and smooth the surface.
  4. Bake for about 50-60 minutes, until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  5. Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and leave the cheesecake inside for 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the cheesecake, let it cool completely, and then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
Raspberry Mousse:
  1. Soak the gelatin in cold water for 5 minutes.
  2. Blend the raspberries with powdered sugar and lemon juice. If using fresh raspberries, strain the mixture to remove the seeds.
  3. Squeeze excess water from the gelatin, dissolve it in a small amount of warm water, and then mix it with the raspberry mixture.
  4. Whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form and gently fold it into the raspberry mixture.
  5. Pour the raspberry mousse over the chilled cheesecake and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  6. Before serving, garnish with fresh raspberries and mint leaves.

Tips

  • All cheese filling ingredients should be at room temperature to avoid lumps forming.
  • To prevent the cheesecake from cracking during baking, use low temperature and don't open the oven door while baking.
  • It's best to prepare the cheesecake a day ahead - the chilling time allows the flavors to fully develop.
  • If using frozen raspberries, you don't need to thaw them before blending.
  • It's worth placing the baking pan in a larger dish filled with hot water (water bath) - this helps in even baking and prevents cracking.

Variations

Lemon Version

Replace the raspberry mousse with lemon curd - a lemon cream with added lemon zest for intense aroma.

Oreo Cookie Base

For a flavor contrast, use chocolate cookies like Oreos for the crust instead of butter cookies or graham crackers.

Gluten-Free Version

Use gluten-free cookies and cornstarch instead of potato starch for a gluten-free version.

Apple-Cinnamon Mousse

A fall alternative is to replace the raspberry mousse with apple-cinnamon mousse.
