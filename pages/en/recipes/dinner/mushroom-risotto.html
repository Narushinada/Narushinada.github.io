Creamy Mushroom Risotto

45 min 4 servings 420 kcal Dinner Vegetarian

Italian Classic in Your Kitchen

Mushroom risotto is one of the most characteristic dishes of Italian cuisine. The creamy consistency, intense mushroom flavor, and aromatic herbs create an amazing combination that will satisfy even the most demanding gourmets.

The key to success is patience and proper technique - slowly adding stock and constantly stirring the rice allows its natural starch to be released, which gives risotto its characteristic creaminess. The dish can be served as a meal on its own or as a side dish to meat courses.

Ingredients

  • Risotto rice (arborio/carnaroli) 320 g
  • Fresh mushrooms 250 g
  • Onion 1 small
  • Garlic 2 cloves
  • Dry white wine 120 ml
  • Stock 1 liter
  • Butter 50 g
  • Parmesan cheese 60 g
  • Olive oil 2 tablespoons
  • Thyme 2 tbsp fresh
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley 2 tablespoons

Instructions

  1. Prepare the mushrooms - clean them and slice them.
  2. In a large, deep pot or pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes until they brown and release their moisture. Remove them and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, heat the remaining olive oil. Add the chopped onion and cook over medium heat for about 3 minutes until soft and translucent. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the rice to the pot and stir for 1-2 minutes until the grains become slightly translucent at the edges.
  5. Pour in the wine and stir until it is completely absorbed.
  6. Add the hot stock, a ladleful at a time, stirring and waiting for the liquid to be absorbed by the rice before adding the next portion. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes.
  7. When the rice is almost tender (al dente), add the cooked mushrooms and thyme, stirring gently.
  8. Remove the pot from the heat and add the butter and grated parmesan. Stir until the risotto becomes creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Cover the pot and let it rest for 2 minutes.
  10. Serve hot, sprinkled with chopped parsley and additional parmesan.

Tips

  • The best mushrooms for risotto are porcini, brown button mushrooms, or white button mushrooms. You can also use a mix of mushrooms for a richer flavor.
  • Risotto rice should be of a special kind - arborio or carnaroli, which have a high starch content.
  • The stock should be hot when adding it to the rice - this is important for the proper cooking process.
  • Don't wash the rice before cooking - the starch on the surface of the grains is essential for achieving a creamy consistency.
  • The final addition of butter and parmesan (called mantecatura) is crucial for achieving the perfect, creamy texture of risotto.

Serving Suggestions

Wine

Mushroom risotto pairs perfectly with a dry white wine, such as Pinot Grigio or Chardonnay.

Salad

Serve with a light arugula salad drizzled with lemon juice and olive oil.

Meat

As a side dish, risotto pairs wonderfully with grilled chicken or tender veal.

Cheese

Serve with additional parmesan for sprinkling and possibly a few drops of truffle oil.
